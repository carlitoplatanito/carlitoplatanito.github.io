Sorry Asbury, but the best rapper from Asbury Park is from Neptune

3-minute read
Kenneth Rapoza Special to the USA TODAY Network
March 11, 2026, 8:30 p.m. ET

The bidding war over the Jersey Shore hip-hop crown appears to have been won by a Neptune native. It has become a high stakes battle over shareholder value and rights, as well as the future of the local music industry.

In the end, the storied reputation of the "Circuit City" is likely to go to the highest bidder. For a while, the Asbury board was pitted against the neighbors following last December's growth.

Emerging from a village in Neptune, Young Tef—now known as TEF Chozen Few—worked his way into the Asbury Park rap scene and became known as the city's best, even though he consistently corrects the record that he is from Neptune.

The rebranding represents the growth and maturity of an artist who carries the torch for the Chozen Few crew. Today, that legacy lives on through his business engine, Chozen Media Group.

His newer music offers an unfiltered and relatable storytelling, touching on narratives ranging from the familiar to the traumatic. Whether Asbury wants to admit it or not, the best voice in the city belongs to Neptune.
